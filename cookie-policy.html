01 COOKIE BASICS

WHAT ARE COOKIES?

Cookies are small pieces of information that a website may store through your browser or device. They can be used to support functionality, remember settings or help website operators understand how services are being used.

Cookies may exist for different periods. Some may operate only during a browsing session, while others may remain for a defined period unless removed earlier.

02 SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES

BEYOND TRADITIONAL COOKIES

Websites may use technologies other than traditional browser cookies to store information, access device data or support website and measurement functionality.

References to cookies in this policy may also include similar technologies where appropriate to the context.

03 PURPOSE

WHY TECHNOLOGIES MAY BE USED

ARVERSE may use cookies or similar technologies where reasonably required to operate the website, protect systems, remember settings, understand website activity or support measurement and marketing systems.

The technologies actually used may change as the website, services and external integrations evolve.

04 ESSENTIAL

CORE WEBSITE TECHNOLOGIES

Some technologies may be required for basic website functionality, security, network management, consent preference storage or features specifically requested by the visitor.

CORE FUNCTION

Blocking essential technologies may affect the operation of certain website features or services.

05 ANALYTICS

UNDERSTANDING WEBSITE ACTIVITY

Analytics technologies, where implemented, may help ARVERSE understand information such as general traffic patterns, page activity, device categories and website performance.

This information may be used to understand how the website performs and identify opportunities to improve content or user experience.

06 MARKETING

CAMPAIGN AND ATTRIBUTION SYSTEMS

Where advertising or campaign measurement systems are used, relevant technologies may support conversion measurement, attribution, campaign analysis or audience-related functions.

The specific technologies used will depend on the marketing platforms connected to the website at that time.

07 THIRD PARTIES

EXTERNAL SERVICE PROVIDERS

Some website functionality may depend on third-party service providers. These providers may set or access technologies according to their own services and policies.

Third-party services may include website infrastructure, analytics platforms, embedded services, advertising systems or communication tools.

THIRD-PARTY CONTROL

External providers operate under their own policies, technical systems and retention practices.

08 USER CONTROLS

MANAGING YOUR CHOICES

Depending on the technologies in use, you may be able to control optional cookies through the website's consent interface, browser settings or relevant third-party controls.

Browser settings may allow you to inspect, block or delete stored cookies. Blocking certain technologies may affect some website features.

09 POLICY UPDATES

CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

This Cookie Policy may be updated when website functionality, integrations, analytics tools, marketing systems or legal requirements change.

The current published version will apply from the date it becomes available on the website.

10 CONTACT

QUESTIONS ABOUT COOKIES?

Questions regarding this Cookie Policy or the technologies used by ARVERSE may be submitted through the official contact channel.
